Rewards & Risks

Rewards

Ubiquitous CPU architecture — ARM designs are in ~99% of smartphones and are expanding fast into the data center (Graviton, Cobalt, Axion, Grace)
FY26 record year: revenue $4.92B +23% YoY, 3rd straight year of >20% growth; royalty $2.61B (+21%), licensing $2.31B (+25%)
Armv9 + Compute Subsystems (CSS) lift royalty rate ~2x per chip vs v8 — a structural, multi-year royalty tailwind independent of unit volume
Data-center royalties more than doubled in FY26 and are guided to double again in FY27 — the AI custom-silicon wave runs through ARM IP
Capital-light model: ~97.5% gross margin, ~$3.1B net cash, positive FCF — near-zero balance-sheet risk
Long-term ambition: $15B chip-related revenue and EPS >$9 by ~2031 if the data-center + royalty-rate story compounds

Risks

Valuation is extreme — ~321x trailing / ~123x forward earnings on only ~23% revenue growth; priced for a decade of flawless execution, negative margin of safety
SoftBank owns ~90% — tiny ~10% free float means thin liquidity, a persistent supply overhang if SoftBank sells, and weak minority-holder governance
RISC-V, the royalty-free open ISA, is a rising alternative; Qualcomm and others are exploring it and even ARM licensees fund RISC-V hedges — long-run pricing-power threat
ARM China — a separately-controlled entity ARM does not fully consolidate — is a large slice of revenue and a recurring governance / geopolitical wildcard
Licensing revenue is lumpy and ARM is moving toward designing its own chips (competing with customers); FY27 Q1 guide ($1.26B) sits below Q4's $1.49B record
