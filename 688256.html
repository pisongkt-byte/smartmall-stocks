Rewards & Risks

Rewards

First-ever annual profit in 2025: revenue ¥6.50B (+453%), net ¥2.06B; Q1'26 revenue ¥2.88B (+160% YoY)
Prime beneficiary of forced domestic AI-chip substitution — China localization 17% (2023) → 55% (2027E) as Nvidia H20/H200 access is throttled
Only listed pure-play domestic AI-accelerator designer (Siyuan 590/690) — scarcity premium + national-champion status

55% gross margin with strong operating leverage (op margin ~43% TTM) as R&D spreads over a far bigger revenue base
2026 target ~500K accelerators (>3x 2025) with firm telco/finance/internet/state-cloud orders; TCO ~30–60% below Nvidia. Buyable in baht via Thai DR CAMBRI80

Risks

Extreme valuation — 312x trailing / ~70x fwd P/E, 66x P/B; even bulls say the price discounts ~3 years of flawless growth with no margin of safety. Any stumble re-rates it violently.
Supply is the binding constraint, not demand — the 500K ramp depends on scarce SMIC N+2 (7nm) capacity (shared with, and behind, Huawei) and limited HBM; low yields could cap output regardless of orders.
Cash-flow quality — FY2025 OCF −¥0.5B and FCF −¥1.1B despite ¥2.06B 'profit', on a ¥4.9B inventory pile; unsold inventory carries writedown risk.
US Entity List / export controls — tighter EDA-tool, IP or foundry restrictions could choke design or manufacturing access.
Competition — Huawei Ascend (larger, vertically integrated) plus Hygon/Biren/Moore Threads, and a possible Nvidia H200 China re-entry recapturing premium demand.
Concentration & policy dependence — revenue is >99% cloud accelerators tied to lumpy, tender-based state-directed AI capex, making quarters volatile.
