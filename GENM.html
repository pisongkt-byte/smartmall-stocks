Risks

Elevated leverage — net debt RM12.8B (~4x EBITDA) plus a US$5B+ RWNYC build-out could pressure the balance sheet and force a deeper dividend cut.
Empire Resorts (US) remains loss-making and cash-consumptive; S&P warns GENM may have to keep funding it — a structural drag with minority-interest complications.
US regulatory/reputational overhang: parent-owned Resorts World Las Vegas paid a US$10.5M Nevada AML fine and faces a federal RICO civil suit — group licensing/goodwill risk.
Earnings quality — a big chunk of the FY25 jump was an UNREALISED forex gain on USD debt, not operations; it reverses if the ringgit weakens.
Dividend on a downtrend — cut 15 → 10 → 7 sen over three years; real but not growing.
Timing/execution — table games + tax benefit are H2-2026 events; soft US consumer or post-2030 NYC saturation caps upside. No Thai DR — needs an offshore/global equity desk.
