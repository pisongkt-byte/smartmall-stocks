What It Does
The world's largest mobile carrier — ~1 billion mobile subscribers and ~315M wireline-broadband homes (~50% of China's market). State-controlled (China Mobile Communications Group owns ~70%), it sells personal mobile/data plans, home broadband bundles, and a fast-growing enterprise 'DICT' stack (cloud, computing, AI, IoT). Service revenue is ~85% of the top line; the rest is low-margin handset/product sales.
How it makes money
Personal Market~47%Mobile voice + 5G data plans for ~1B subscribers — the mature cash engine, flat-to-low growth
Gov't & Enterprise (DICT)~22%Cloud, computing, AI, IoT, private 5G — the growth driver; computing services RMB89.8B (+11%)
Home Market~14%Wireline broadband + smart-home bundles; ~315M households, +7% YoY
Product sales~17%Handset / device resale — low-margin pass-through, not a profit driver

Rewards & Risks

Rewards

Dirt cheap for the quality — ~11x earnings / ~1.05x book / ~6% yield on a monopoly-scale, profitable carrier
Net-cash fortress (~RMB297B, negligible debt) fully funds the dividend AND DICT capex with zero solvency risk
Rising-payout policy (to 75% by FY26, ~14% 3-yr div CAGR) turns a flat earnings base into a growing income stream
DICT / computing / AI is a genuine bright spot — computing services +11%, digital now 20% of principal revenue
Utility-like stability: ~1B mobile subs + ~315M broadband homes (~50% share); revenue barely moves through cycles

Risks

STATE-OWNED, LOW-GROWTH BY DESIGN: ~70% owned by the state, China Mobile is run for policy and stability, not shareholder maximization — total revenue +0.9% and profit −0.9% in FY2025. The huge idle cash pile could be directed into low-return, state-mandated investment instead of dividends/buybacks; this governance overhang is the single biggest reason the stock is cheap and the biggest swing factor on the thesis
GEOPOLITICS / DELISTING HISTORY: China Mobile's US ADRs were forcibly delisted from the NYSE in 2021 under US executive order, and it sits on US investment blacklists — a tail risk that can hit sentiment and liquidity, and a reason no clean US/Thai access route exists
Structurally flat core: the personal/mobile market is saturated and price-competitive among the three state carriers; DICT is real but too small to re-accelerate a RMB1.05T top line — capital appreciation likely lags the dividend, so most of the return is the ~6% yield
