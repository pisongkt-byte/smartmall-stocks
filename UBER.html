What It Does
The world's largest mobility + delivery platform — a two-sided marketplace matching riders and eaters with drivers and couriers across 70+ countries, taking a cut of every transaction. After years of losses it now runs profitably on ~$11B free cash flow.

Delivery33%Uber Eats food + grocery/retail plus high-margin Uber One membership (>50M members) — the fastest grower

Rewards & Risks

Rewards

Q1-26 reaccelerated, not decelerated — bookings +25%, Delivery +28%, GAAP op income +57%, adj EBITDA +33%, FCF +66% YoY; Q2 guide ahead of consensus and Freight back to growth
Cheap now — ~19x fwd / ~17x trailing / ~2.6x sales / ~13x P/FCF / PEG ~0.68 after the further slide to ~$70, ~32% off $102 highs
Profitability inflection is real and durable — $5.6B FY25 GAAP operating income (~2x YoY) + ~$11B run-rate free cash flow
Dominant two-sided network: Mobility 57% of FY25 rev + Delivery bookings now ≈ Mobility size; Uber One >50M members + advertising add high-margin layers
$20B buyback authorization (first-ever) funded by the FCF engine — share-count return story already shrinking the float as price keeps drifting

Risks

AUTONOMOUS VEHICLES are an existential two-sided bet — if Waymo/Tesla robotaxis disintermediate the driver network, Uber's core Mobility economics could be hollowed out; if Uber becomes the demand aggregator across AV operators, it's a moat. The market can't price this, and it's the single biggest swing factor — and the source of the ~32% drawdown from $102 to ~$70 even as fundamentals reaccelerated
TTM net income (~$8.5B / ~16% margin) is still partially flattered by the FY25 ~$5B one-off Dutch deferred-tax benefit — run-rate earnings closer to $6–7B; don't anchor to the 17x trailing P/E
Pricing-practice investigations ('dramatically different' prices for same rides) + persistent gig-economy driver-classification / regulatory risk across US/EU; reported revenue growth (+14% in Q1) ran ~9pp behind bookings due to business-model accounting shifts
