Rewards & Risks

Rewards

Trades ~56% below ~RM5.00 RNAV and at 0.29x book — its 52.6% Genting Singapore stake (~RM12.6B) alone exceeds GENT's RM8.4B market cap
Resorts World NYC won a full downstate NY casino license (Dec 2025) — a multi-decade earnings driver via 73%-owned Genting Malaysia
Genting Singapore RWS 2.0 expansion ramping into 2026–27, self-funded by ~S$3B net cash
Kasuri FLNG (1.2 MTPA) targets first gas ~2027, +~RM660M/yr EBITDA to the wholly-owned energy arm
Raised its Genting Malaysia stake to ~73% in 2025 — consolidates more of the US/UK recovery + NY upside into the holdco
Near multi-year lows (RM2.18 vs 52w low RM2.16) with washed-out sentiment and MY+SG tourism tailwinds

Risks

Perpetual holdco discount — Genting has traded 40–60% below RNAV for over a decade with no structural catalyst to collapse the structure, release GENS cash, or buy back GENT; it can persist indefinitely (value trap).
Capital allocation has repeatedly failed to close the discount — cash went to low-return RWLV, idle Miami land, loss-making Empire, and a debt-funded GENM buy-up rather than buying back deeply discounted GENT stock.
RNAV hinges on unlisted RWLV + Miami being worth well above the ~RM21B / US$5B holdco net debt against them; if they're worth ~their debt, the 'huge discount' largely disappears (RNAV falls to ~RM1.4–3.0).
High holdco leverage — ~RM2.1B/yr net interest left parent PATAMI at ~breakeven in FY2025; most group profit belongs to the subs' minorities.
Dividend cut to ~5 sen (2.3% yield) signals cash prioritised for M&A and debt over shareholders.
Cyclical/regulatory — gaming-tax changes (MY/SG), VIP/Chinese-tourist softness, CPO price cycle, US casino execution/capex risk. No Thai DR on any Genting entity.
